Overview

Executive Summary

CTS already has a proven foundation: ONA data that maps informal organizational networks, a certified ServiceNow integration, and a patent-pending Modular AI Agent Framework. What's missing is a closed-loop, action-oriented recommendation system that takes those network insights and automatically surfaces the right mentor and the right cross-team collaborator for each employee — with HR workflows to act on those recommendations in ServiceNow without manual analysis.

This proposal outlines SmartMatch, a two-module recommendation workflow layer built on top of ONA by CTS & ServiceNow — turning insight dashboards into automated HR actions.

Module 1

MentorMatch — Intelligent Mentor Pairing

Module 1 MentorMatch

Automatically recommends the top 3 mentor candidates for each employee, ranked by network compatibility, skill complementarity, and availability signals — removing the manual guesswork from HR.

Data Inputs (Active + Passive ONA)

Network graph (ONA) Skill gap signals (Growth & Dev) Collaboration frequency (passive) Past mentorship outcomes Meeting/email patterns

Recommendation Scoring

MentorScore = α · NetworkBridgeScore
            + β · SkillComplementarityScore
            + γ · AvailabilityScore
            + δ · DiversityScore (dept, tenure, geography)

Weights (α, β, γ, δ) are tunable per organization's goals — e.g., M&A mode weights NetworkBridgeScore higher to break acquired-company silos.

ServiceNow Workflow

  1. 1

    Trigger: New hire starts onboarding in Employee Journey Management, or employee enrolls in a Growth & Development plan

  2. 2

    SmartMatch agent queries ONA graph → generates ranked mentor list with plain-language explanations

  3. 3

    HR receives a ServiceNow task with ranked list, a "Why this mentor?" card, and approve/swap actions

  4. 4

    Upon approval, ServiceNow auto-sends introduction messages, schedules first meeting, and creates a mentorship record

  5. 5

    Check-in nudges at 30/60/90 days with a feedback loop that retrains the model over time

Module 2

TeamBridge — Cross-Team Collaboration Recommendations

Module 2 TeamBridge

Proactively surfaces employees in other teams who share relevant project contexts, complementary expertise, or are structural bridges — turning ONA's structural hole detection into actionable "People You Should Know" suggestions.

Data Inputs

Structural hole detection (ONA) Project/ticket metadata (ServiceNow) Collaboration velocity (passive ONA) Employee-stated interests (Active ONA) Skills tags (Growth & Dev)

Two Recommendation Modes

ModeUse CaseAlgorithm
Serendipity Mode Innovation, knowledge sharing Collaborative filtering on project tag overlap + network distance
Targeted Mode Specific skill gap or project need Content-based filtering on skill profiles + structural hole bridging

ServiceNow Workflow

  1. 1

    Trigger: Weekly digest, or event-driven (new project started, role change, onboarding milestone)

  2. 2

    SmartMatch agent queries ONA graph → generates "People You Should Know" cards with context ("Alex in Engineering solved a similar problem in Q3")

  3. 3

    Delivered via ServiceNow Employee Center as a widget or push notification

  4. 4

    Employee can one-click request a warm intro (HR or shared manager notified), or save for later

  5. 5

    Opt-in office hours scheduling: if recommended collaborator has flagged availability, direct calendar booking enabled

Ethics & Compliance

Privacy & Ethical Design

Given that passive ONA involves behavioral signal collection, three guardrails are essential:

  1. 1

    Aggregate-only passive signals — individual email/meeting data is never exposed; only graph-level scores surface in recommendations

  2. 2

    Explainable recommendations — every suggestion shows a plain-language reason ("Based on your skills gap in cloud architecture and Maria's expertise flagged in your growth plan")

  3. 3

    Employee opt-out — employees can opt out of cross-team discovery mode; mentorship matching remains HR-driven
